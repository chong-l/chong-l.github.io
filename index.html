Chong Liu @ UC Santa Barbara

 

Welcome to my homepage! I'm Chong Liu (刘冲), a Ph.D. Candidate in Computer Science advised by Prof. Yu-Xiang Wang at University of California, Santa Barbara.

In 2018, I received my M.S. in Computer Science advised by Prof. Yuan Jiang in LAMDA Group, Nanjing University, Nanjing, China. In 2015, I received my B.E. in Electrical Engineering from Northwestern Polytechnical University, Xi'an, China. From Aug. 2014 to Feb. 2015, I spent six months visiting Hanyang University, Ansan, South Korea.

I'm an editorial board reviewer of the Journal of Machine Learning Research (JMLR).

News

  • Jun. 2022: Two papers accepted by the ICML Workshop on Adaptive Experimental Design and Active Learning in the Real World Workshop (ReALML-2022)! See you in Baltimore, MD in July!
  • Apr. 2022: Happy to be invited to speak at RPI CS Theory Seminar!
  • Jan. 2022: Paper "Doubly Robust Crowdsourcing" has been published in the Journal of Artificial Intelligence Research (JAIR)! In this paper, crowdsourcing test-time label aggregation is formulated as a statistical estimation problem of inferring the expected voting score. Doubly robust estimator is used to improve crowdsourcing accuracy and save labeling cost at the same time.
  • Nov. 2021: Paper "Revisiting Model-Agnostic Private Learning: Faster Rates and Active Learning" has been published in the Journal of Machine Learning Research (JMLR)! In this paper, active learning-based algorithm PATE-ASQ is able work in practice while saving privacy budget at the same time under the differential privacy framework.

Research Interests

My research interests include global optimization, active learning, and ensemble learning, as well as their applications in hyperparameter tuning and material design.
